# **Review and Publish Phase**

## **DEC Learning Hub Course Creator Portal Core Workflow Specification**

## **A. What the Review and Publish Phase Is**

The **Review and Publish Phase** is the formal quality assurance, approval, and release stage of the DEC Learning Hub Course Creator Portal. It begins after the **Build-to-Review Handover Record** has been approved in the Build Phase.

This phase ensures that a course is not published simply because it has been built. Before a course becomes available to learners, it must pass structured review for:

- alignment with the approved Analysis and Design records;

- relevance to the CSO capacity gap;

- quality of content and learner experience;

- final test validity;

- certificate logic;

- practical output and verified achievement logic, where applicable;

- safeguarding, civic-space, and data protection safety;

- accessibility, localization, and low-bandwidth readiness;

- AI-generated content quality;

- platform functionality;

- publication readiness.

The Review and Publish Phase answers:

> Is this course accurate, safe, practical, accessible, aligned with DEC’s CSO capacity-strengthening purpose, and ready for learners?

It also ensures that publishing is **role-sensitive**. Course creators may submit a course for review, revise it, and respond to reviewer comments, but they should not directly publish courses to the learner-facing platform unless they are assigned an authorized publishing role. Publication should be controlled by DEC admins or authorized reviewers.

## **B. Why the Review and Publish Phase Is Needed**

The Review and Publish Phase is needed because a course can pass through Analysis, Design, and Build and still contain issues that should be fixed before learners access it.

Common risks include:

- the course has become too generic;

- added blocks expanded the course beyond the approved scope;

- AI-generated text introduced unsupported claims;

- final test questions are unclear, unfair, or not aligned with the course;

- practical proof submission asks for unsafe information;

- certificate and verified achievement logic are confused;

- media is too heavy for low-bandwidth learners;

- scenarios include civic-space or safeguarding risks;

- language is too technical for grassroots CSO learners;

- the learner journey is confusing;

- the course does not function properly in preview mode.

This phase protects DEC, learners, partner CSOs, and communities. It ensures that published courses are not only technically complete, but also **credible, safe, useful, and aligned with real CSO capacity strengthening**.

## **C. Who Does What in the Review and Publish Phase**

| **Actor**                             | **Role**                                                                                                                                             |
|---------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Course creator                        | Submits the built course for review, responds to reviewer comments, revises the course, and resubmits when needed.                                   |
| Instructional design reviewer         | Checks learning flow, block quality, practice logic, assessment alignment, learner workload, and clarity.                                            |
| Subject matter expert                 | Checks technical accuracy, CSO relevance, indicator alignment, examples, and final test answer keys.                                                 |
| DEC capacity lead                     | Confirms the course remains linked to the original CSO capacity objective, performance goal, and indicator/standard.                                 |
| Safeguarding / civic-space reviewer   | Reviews sensitive scenarios, advocacy guidance, data protection, proof submission, and do-no-harm issues.                                            |
| Accessibility / localization reviewer | Checks mobile-first layout, low-bandwidth readiness, readability, alt text, transcript, translation-readiness, and inclusive language.               |
| Platform/admin reviewer               | Checks course settings, final test logic, certificate trigger, practical output workflow, badge configuration, permissions, and publishing metadata. |
| Authorized publisher / DEC admin      | Makes the final publish decision and releases the course to the learner-facing platform.                                                             |
| AI assistant                          | May help summarize reviewer comments or suggest edits, but cannot approve or publish the course.                                                     |

# **D. Step-by-Step Review Workflow**

## **Step 1: Open the Build-to-Review Handover Record**

The Review Phase begins with the approved **Build-to-Review Handover Record**.

The reviewer should see:

- course title;

- target learner group;

- CSO capacity area;

- performance goal;

- original capacity gap;

- required and added blocks;

- AI use summary;

- final test and certificate configuration;

- practical output and verified achievement pathway;

- safeguarding and data protection checks;

- accessibility and low-bandwidth checks;

- learner preview record;

- reviewer attention list.

This record becomes the main review dashboard.

## **Step 2: Check traceability to Analysis and Design**

The first review question is whether the built course still follows the approved logic.

The reviewer checks:

| **Traceability question**                                                      | **Yes/No** |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Does the course still respond to the approved CSO capacity gap?                |            |
| Does it match the approved target learner group?                               |            |
| Does it support the approved performance goal?                                 |            |
| Does it remain linked to the correct CSO capacity area and indicator/standard? |            |
| Does it stay within the approved Knowledge/Skill scope for Phase 1?            |            |
| Does it avoid Motivation or Environment gaps as course drivers?                |            |
| Does it preserve the evaluation and recognition anchor?                        |            |

If the course has drifted from the approved Analysis or Design record, it should return to Build or Design for correction.

## **Step 3: Review required and added blocks**

Because the Build Phase allows flexible block addition, reviewers must distinguish between:

- required blocks from the approved storyboard;

- recommended blocks;

- creator-added blocks.

The reviewer checks whether each added block is justified and useful.

| **Added block review question**                                                                                                                   | **Yes/No** |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Is each added block linked to a valid purpose?                                                                                                    |            |
| Does each added block improve clarity, practice, accessibility, localization, safety, navigation, test readiness, or practical output completion? |            |
| Does any added block introduce unrelated content?                                                                                                 |            |
| Does any added block make the course too long or theory-heavy?                                                                                    |            |
| Should any added block be removed, shortened, or moved to optional resources?                                                                     |            |

The goal is not to punish creators for improving the course. The goal is to ensure flexibility does not become scope drift.

## **Step 4: Review learning content quality**

The reviewer checks whether the learner-facing content is clear, practical, and appropriate for local CSO learners.

| **Content quality question**                                                                                    | **Yes/No** |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Is the language simple and learner-facing?                                                                      |            |
| Does the course avoid unnecessary theory?                                                                       |            |
| Are explanations short and practical?                                                                           |            |
| Are examples relevant to local CSO realities?                                                                   |            |
| Does content avoid generic corporate-style training language?                                                   |            |
| Does every major content block support a required action, test item, practical output, or learner support need? |            |
| Are templates, checklists, examples, or job aids used where helpful?                                            |            |
| Is learner workload realistic?                                                                                  |            |

## **Step 5: Review AI-assisted content**

If AI was used, the reviewer checks the AI-Assisted Content Drafting and Review Log.

| **AI review question**                                                       | **Yes/No** |
|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Was all AI-generated content reviewed by a human?                            |            |
| Were inaccurate, generic, unsafe, or unsupported outputs rejected or edited? |            |
| Did AI avoid inventing donor rules, legal advice, indicators, or objectives? |            |
| Did AI avoid real beneficiary cases or sensitive details?                    |            |
| Did AI-generated examples remain fictionalized and context-appropriate?      |            |
| Did AI-generated final test items align with approved course content?        |            |
| Are any AI outputs still requiring SME or safeguarding review?               |            |

No AI-generated content should be published without human review.

## **Step 6: Review practice activities and scenarios**

For Skill-focused courses, reviewers check whether practice activities are realistic, safe, and useful.

| **Practice/scenario review question**                                    | **Yes/No** |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Does each activity practice a real CSO task?                             |            |
| Are scenarios realistic for the target learner group?                    |            |
| Are choices plausible, not artificially obvious?                         |            |
| Do consequences explain practical effects, not only “correct/incorrect”? |            |
| Are templates or practice outputs clear?                                 |            |
| Are instructions short enough for mobile learners?                       |            |
| Are safeguarding, data protection, or civic-space risks addressed?       |            |

Practice activities should strengthen CSO performance, not merely decorate the course.

## **Step 7: Review final test, pass rule, and certificate logic**

Every course should have a final test.

The reviewer checks whether the test is fair, relevant, and correctly configured.

| **Final test review question**                                                            | **Yes/No** |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Is the final test included?                                                               |            |
| Does it assess only content covered in the course?                                        |            |
| Does it test essential knowledge and decision logic, not unnecessary theory?              |            |
| Are question types appropriate: MCQ, true/false, matching, sequencing, or scenario-based? |            |
| Are answer keys correct and SME-verified?                                                 |            |
| Are distractors plausible but not misleading?                                             |            |
| Are feedback messages helpful?                                                            |            |
| Is the pass threshold set at 80%?                                                         |            |
| Is the automated certificate threshold set at 90%?                                        |            |
| Is the learner clearly told the difference between passing and certificate eligibility?   |            |
| Is retake logic configured?                                                               |            |

The platform rule should remain:

| **Learner result** | **Platform outcome**                           |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Below 80%          | Not passed; review and retake recommended      |
| 80% to 89%         | Course passed; no automated certificate yet    |
| 90% or above       | Course passed and automated certificate issued |

The certificate should confirm strong final test performance. It should not claim that the learner has demonstrated verified workplace application.

## **Step 8: Review practical output and verified achievement pathway**

Where the course includes practical proof submission, reviewers check whether the pathway is clear, safe, and linked to CSO capacity standards.

| **Practical output review question**                                     | **Yes/No/NA** |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|
| Is the practical output clearly explained?                               |               |
| Are accepted proof types defined?                                        |               |
| Is the review rubric simple and practical?                               |               |
| Is the proof linked to a CSO capacity area and indicator/standard?       |               |
| Is the reviewer role defined?                                            |               |
| Is the verified achievement or badge label clear?                        |               |
| Is verified achievement clearly separate from the automated certificate? |               |
| Are anonymization and data safety instructions included?                 |               |
| Are donor-facing recognition settings safe and consent-based?            |               |

The platform should preserve this distinction:

> The final test confirms course learning. The automated certificate confirms high final test performance. The verified achievement or badge confirms reviewed practical proof linked to CSO capacity indicators or standards.

## **Step 9: Review safeguarding, civic-space, and data protection**

This review is mandatory for courses involving safeguarding, advocacy, accountability, beneficiary data, community feedback, government relations, or sensitive civic-space issues.

| **Safety review question**                                                 | **Yes/No/NA** |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|
| Does the course avoid asking learners to disclose sensitive personal data? |               |
| Are real names, beneficiary details, and identifiable cases avoided?       |               |
| Are sensitive examples fictionalized or anonymized?                        |               |
| Are proof submission instructions safe?                                    |               |
| Are advocacy or civic-space scenarios framed carefully?                    |               |
| Does the course avoid unsupported legal advice?                            |               |
| Are safeguarding referral instructions accurate and safe?                  |               |
| Is specialist review required before publication?                          |               |

If a course creates safeguarding, data, or civic-space risks, it should not be published until corrected.

## **Step 10: Review accessibility, localization, and low-bandwidth readiness**

The reviewer checks whether the course is realistic for local CSO learners.

| **Accessibility/localization question**                   | **Yes/No** |
|-----------------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Is the course mobile-friendly?                            |            |
| Are sections short and readable?                          |            |
| Is the language plain and translation-ready?              |            |
| Is jargon explained or removed?                           |            |
| Are images given alt text or text alternatives?           |            |
| Are audio/video materials given transcript or captions?   |            |
| Are file sizes low-bandwidth friendly?                    |            |
| Are downloadable resources lightweight?                   |            |
| Are printable or offline resources included where needed? |            |
| Are examples locally relevant and inclusive?              |            |
| Is the total course time realistic?                       |            |

Low-bandwidth readiness should be treated as a core publication requirement, not an optional improvement.

## **Step 11: Review learner preview and platform functionality**

The reviewer or platform admin checks the course in learner mode.

| **Platform functionality check**                   | **Yes/No** |
|----------------------------------------------------|------------|
| Course opens correctly in learner view             |            |
| Navigation works correctly                         |            |
| Required and added blocks display correctly        |            |
| Interactions work correctly                        |            |
| Scenario logic works correctly                     |            |
| Final test works correctly                         |            |
| Score calculation works correctly                  |            |
| Certificate trigger works at 90%+                  |            |
| Practical proof submission works, if applicable    |            |
| Verified achievement workflow works, if applicable |            |
| Downloads work correctly                           |            |
| Progress tracking works correctly                  |            |
| Course completion status works correctly           |            |

## **Step 12: Return for revision or approve for publish**

After review, the course receives one of the following decisions:

| **Review decision**        | **Meaning**                                                                         |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Approved for publish       | Course is ready for publication                                                     |
| Approved with minor fixes  | Course can publish after specified minor edits                                      |
| Return to Build            | Course needs content, block, test, accessibility, or platform fixes                 |
| Return to Design           | Course has drifted from approved design or needs design-level changes               |
| Return to Analysis         | Capacity gap, learner group, indicator, or baseline issue must be revisited         |
| Specialist review required | Safeguarding, civic-space, legal, data, or technical concern requires expert review |
| Not approved               | Course should not proceed in current form                                           |

# **E. Step-by-Step Publish Workflow**

## **Step 1: Confirm publish approval**

Only courses with an approved review decision should move to Publish.

Before publishing, the platform should confirm:

- all required review checks are complete;

- unresolved reviewer comments are closed;

- certificate logic is tested;

- practical output logic is tested, if applicable;

- safeguarding and accessibility checks are complete;

- publishing metadata is complete;

- authorized publisher has approved.

## **Step 2: Complete publishing metadata**

The publisher or admin completes final metadata for learner-facing display.

| **Metadata field**                   | **Entry**                                                 |
|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| Course title                         |                                                           |
| Short description                    |                                                           |
| Target learners                      |                                                           |
| CSO capacity area                    |                                                           |
| Related indicator / standard         |                                                           |
| Course level                         | Introductory / Intermediate / Advanced                    |
| Estimated time                       |                                                           |
| Language(s)                          |                                                           |
| Delivery mode                        | Mobile-first / Low-bandwidth / Online / Offline-supported |
| Certificate eligibility              | 90%+ final test score                                     |
| Practical output / badge pathway     | Available / Not available                                 |
| Verified achievement linked to       | Indicator / Standard / Organization milestone             |
| Accessibility notes                  |                                                           |
| Safeguarding/data caution, if needed |                                                           |
| Course status                        | Draft / Scheduled / Published / Archived                  |

## **Step 3: Set learner access and visibility**

Publishing should be role-sensitive and audience-sensitive.

| **Access setting**                  | **Options**                                                                    |
|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Visibility                          | Public learner catalog / Restricted cohort / DEC-only / Pilot group / Archived |
| Enrollment                          | Open enrollment / Invitation only / Organization-based / Admin assigned        |
| Target organization(s)              |                                                                                |
| Cohort or program                   |                                                                                |
| Start date                          |                                                                                |
| End date, if any                    |                                                                                |
| Language version                    |                                                                                |
| Certificate enabled?                | Yes / No                                                                       |
| Practical proof enabled?            | Yes / No                                                                       |
| Badge/verified achievement enabled? | Yes / No                                                                       |

For Phase 1, it may be useful to publish some courses first to a **pilot group** before full catalog release.

## **Step 4: Final learner-facing preview**

Before the course goes live, the publisher should complete one final learner-facing preview.

Check:

- catalog card;

- course landing page;

- course description;

- enrollment button;

- module navigation;

- final test;

- certificate rule explanation;

- practical proof explanation;

- mobile display;

- language display;

- accessibility supports;

- completion tracking.

## **Step 5: Publish or schedule publication**

The authorized publisher selects one of the following:

| **Publish action** | **Meaning**                                    |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Publish now        | Course becomes visible immediately             |
| Schedule publish   | Course becomes visible on selected date/time   |
| Publish to pilot   | Course is visible only to selected group       |
| Return to Review   | Course requires final check                    |
| Keep as draft      | Course remains unpublished                     |
| Archive            | Course is removed from active publication path |

Once published, the platform should create a publication record.

## **Step 6: Create publication record**

The publication record should include:

| **Publication field**         | **Entry** |
|-------------------------------|-----------|
| Course ID                     |           |
| Published course title        |           |
| Version number                |           |
| Published by                  |           |
| Publish date                  |           |
| Visibility setting            |           |
| Target learner group          |           |
| Capacity area                 |           |
| Indicator / standard          |           |
| Certificate rule              |           |
| Practical achievement pathway |           |
| Language version              |           |
| Review approval ID            |           |
| Build handover ID             |           |
| Notes / conditions            |           |

## **Step 7: Lock published version and manage revisions**

After publication, the platform should lock the published version.

Future changes should follow version control:

| **Change type**                        | **Required action**                 |
|----------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Typo or minor wording correction       | Minor update log                    |
| Accessibility fix                      | Minor update log and admin approval |
| Final test change                      | Review re-approval required         |
| Certificate rule change                | Admin approval required             |
| Practical proof pathway change         | Review re-approval required         |
| Safeguarding/data safety change        | Specialist review required          |
| Capacity objective or indicator change | Return to Design or Analysis        |
| Major content restructuring            | New version required                |

Each published course should have a version history.

# **F. Platform Design Implications**

The Review and Publish Phase should appear as a structured approval workspace with clear roles, review gates, status tracking, and version control.

## **1. Review Dashboard**

The dashboard should show:

- course summary;

- linked Analysis, Design, and Build records;

- required review areas;

- reviewer assignments;

- review status;

- unresolved comments;

- readiness for publish.

## **2. Multi-Reviewer Workflow**

The platform should support separate review tracks:

- instructional design review;

- SME review;

- DEC capacity alignment review;

- safeguarding/civic-space review;

- accessibility/localization review;

- platform/admin review.

Not every course needs every specialist review, but courses involving sensitive content should trigger the relevant review.

## **3. Inline Commenting and Revision Loop**

Reviewers should be able to comment on:

- blocks;

- final test items;

- scenarios;

- proof submission instructions;

- accessibility issues;

- safeguarding notes;

- metadata;

- certificate and badge settings.

Creators should revise and resubmit without losing the review history.

## **4. Required vs Added Block Review**

Because Build allows flexible block addition, the Review workspace should clearly show:

- required storyboard blocks;

- creator-added blocks;

- justification for added blocks;

- reviewer flags.

## **5. Final Test and Certificate Validation**

The platform should validate:

- test exists;

- score threshold is 80% pass;

- certificate threshold is 90%;

- certificate wording is accurate;

- certificate is separate from verified achievement.

## **6. Practical Achievement Validation**

The platform should validate:

- proof submission instructions;

- review rubric;

- safety/anonymization note;

- badge/verified achievement configuration;

- indicator/standard linkage;

- organization profile linkage, if applicable.

## **7. Accessibility and Safeguarding Gates**

The platform should not allow publish if required safeguarding or accessibility checks are incomplete.

## **8. Role-Sensitive Publish Control**

Creators can submit and revise. Authorized DEC admins or publishers can publish.

## **9. Publication Metadata and Catalog Control**

The platform should manage:

- catalog display;

- course category;

- target learner group;

- visibility;

- enrollment rules;

- language version;

- pilot publication;

- archive status.

## **10. Version Control**

Every published course should have:

- version number;

- publication record;

- change log;

- revision history;

- link to previous version;

- reason for update.

# **Final Refined Tools and Templates for the Review and Publish Phase**

To keep the phase practical, use five tools.

## **Tool 4.1: Course Quality Review Checklist**

**Purpose:** Reviews alignment, content quality, learning flow, block logic, AI use, practice activities, final test, certificate logic, and learner experience.

**Output:** Course quality review record.

## **Tool 4.2: Safeguarding, Accessibility, and Data Safety Review Checklist**

**Purpose:** Reviews civic-space sensitivity, safeguarding risks, proof submission safety, accessibility, localization, and low-bandwidth readiness.

**Output:** Safety and accessibility approval record.

## **Tool 4.3: Final Test, Certificate, and Verified Achievement Review Sheet**

**Purpose:** Confirms final test quality, 80% pass rule, 90% certificate rule, practical proof pathway, badge logic, and distinction between certificate and verified achievement.

**Output:** Assessment and recognition approval record.

## **Tool 4.4: Review-to-Publish Approval Record**

**Purpose:** Consolidates all review decisions and confirms whether the course can move to publication.

**Output:** Approved publish decision or revision decision.

## **Tool 4.5: Publication and Version Control Record**

**Purpose:** Captures publication metadata, visibility, version number, change log, and future revision controls.

**Output:** Published course record.

## **H. Review and Publish Phase Quality Standard**

A course is ready to publish only when it satisfies this standard:

> The course remains traceable to the approved Analysis, Design, and Build records; all required and added blocks are justified and learner-ready; AI-generated content has been reviewed; the final test is valid and configured with 80% pass and 90% certificate logic; practical achievement pathways are safe and indicator-linked where applicable; safeguarding, accessibility, localization, and low-bandwidth checks are complete; the learner preview works; and an authorized publisher approves release.

The Review and Publish Phase preserves the full DEC traceability chain:

> Analysis evidence → capacity gap → baseline → root cause → intervention route → action map → learning design → flexible build → final test → certificate / verified achievement → review → publish → evaluate.
