# **Monitoring and Evaluation Phase**

## **DEC Learning Hub Course Creator Portal Core Workflow Specification**

## **A. What the Monitoring and Evaluation Phase Is**

The **Monitoring and Evaluation Phase** is the final learning, evidence,
and improvement phase of the DEC Learning Hub workflow. It begins after
a course is published and learners start using it.

This phase does not only track whether learners opened or completed a
course. It connects learner activity, final test results, certificates,
practical proof submissions, verified achievements, and learner feedback
back to the original **Analysis Phase baseline** and **Design Phase
performance goal**.

In simple terms:

> The Analysis Phase defines the capacity gap and baseline.  
> The Design Phase defines the performance goal and learner evidence.  
> The Build Phase creates the course, final test, certificate, and
> practical proof pathway.  
> The Review and Publish Phase approves and releases the course.  
> The Monitoring and Evaluation Phase checks whether the course is being
> used, whether learners are passing, whether practical outputs are
> being submitted, and whether the course is contributing to CSO
> capacity strengthening.

This phase should help DEC answer:

- Are learners enrolling, progressing, and completing?

- Are learners passing the final test?

- Are learners earning certificates?

- Are learners submitting practical proof?

- Are verified achievements being awarded?

- Which CSO capacity areas are improving?

- Which courses need revision?

- Which learners or organizations need follow-up support?

- What evidence can DEC use for learning, reporting, donor dialogue, and
  adaptive improvement?

## **B. Why the Monitoring and Evaluation Phase Is Needed**

The Monitoring and Evaluation Phase is needed because the DEC Learning
Hub is not just a content library. It is a capacity-strengthening
platform for CSOs.

A course may be published and completed, but DEC still needs to know
whether it is useful, accessible, relevant, and linked to real CSO
capacity outcomes.

This phase is needed for seven reasons.

First, it **tracks learner engagement and completion**. DEC needs to
know whether learners are starting, progressing, dropping out, passing,
or getting stuck.

Second, it **checks learning achievement** through final test scores.
Since each course includes a final test, DEC can track pass rates,
certificate rates, question difficulty, and areas where learners
struggle.

Third, it **tracks verified practical achievement**. Practical proof
submissions and badges provide stronger evidence of applied CSO capacity
than course completion alone.

Fourth, it **links results to CSO capacity indicators and standards**.
Since courses are mapped to CSO capacity areas and indicators, DEC can
track which capacity areas are being strengthened through the platform.

Fifth, it **supports organizational recognition**. Verified achievements
can help CSOs demonstrate capacity progress to DEC, partners, and
donors, while protecting sensitive raw evidence.

Sixth, it **supports course improvement**. Monitoring data can show
which blocks, test questions, examples, or practical tasks need
revision.

Seventh, it **supports adaptive learning and strategic
decision-making**. DEC can use the data to identify priority support
needs, update course offerings, improve content, and strengthen future
capacity-building strategies.

## **C. Scope of Monitoring and Evaluation in Phase 1**

For Phase 1, Monitoring and Evaluation should remain practical, focused,
and realistic.

The platform should track:

| **Area**             | **Phase 1 focus**                                                  |
|----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Enrollment           | Who enrolled and from which organization, where appropriate        |
| Progress             | Lessons/blocks completed, course progress, drop-off points         |
| Final test           | Attempts, scores, pass rate, certificate eligibility               |
| Certificate          | Automated certificate issued at 90%+ score                         |
| Practical output     | Submission status, review status, accepted/rejected/revision       |
| Verified achievement | Badge or verified achievement awarded after accepted proof         |
| Capacity linkage     | Course linked to CSO capacity area and indicator/standard          |
| Feedback             | Learner feedback on relevance, clarity, usability, and application |
| Course quality       | Issues identified after publication                                |
| Organization profile | Aggregated capacity achievements where safe and appropriate        |

The platform should not claim that a course alone proves full
organizational transformation. Instead, it should distinguish between:

| **Evidence type**                | **Meaning**                                                          |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Enrollment                       | Learner accessed the course                                          |
| Completion                       | Learner finished required course pathway                             |
| Pass status                      | Learner scored 80% or above on final test                            |
| Certificate                      | Learner scored 90% or above on final test                            |
| Practical proof submitted        | Learner or organization submitted evidence of application            |
| Verified achievement             | Practical proof was reviewed and accepted                            |
| Organizational capacity evidence | Aggregated verified achievements suggest progress in a capacity area |

This distinction protects credibility and prevents overclaiming.

## **D. Who Does What in the Monitoring and Evaluation Phase**

| **Actor**                               | **Role**                                                                                      |
|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Learner                                 | Completes course, takes final test, submits practical proof where applicable, gives feedback. |
| Course creator                          | Reviews course analytics and learner feedback to improve course content.                      |
| DEC capacity lead                       | Interprets capacity trends, indicator-linked achievements, and organizational learning needs. |
| Practical output reviewer               | Reviews submitted proof and awards or rejects verified achievements.                          |
| Platform/admin reviewer                 | Monitors system data, certificate logic, badge issuance, and learner progress tracking.       |
| Organization admin, where applicable    | Views aggregated learner progress and verified achievements for their organization.           |
| DEC M&E / learning lead                 | Synthesizes platform evidence for learning, reporting, adaptation, and donor dialogue.        |
| Safeguarding / data protection reviewer | Reviews sensitive submissions, data risks, and evidence-sharing concerns where needed.        |

# **E. Step-by-Step Monitoring and Evaluation Workflow**

## **Step 1: Activate course monitoring after publication**

Once a course is published, the platform should automatically activate
monitoring for that course.

Monitoring should link to:

- course ID;

- course version;

- CSO capacity area;

- related indicator or standard;

- target learner group;

- final test rule;

- certificate rule;

- practical output pathway, if active;

- verified achievement pathway, if active.

This ensures that every course generates data linked to its original
purpose.

## **Step 2: Track enrollment and learner profile data**

The platform should track who is using the course while respecting
privacy and data protection.

Suggested data fields:

| **Data field**                           | **Purpose**                            |
|------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Learner ID                               | Tracks individual learning progress    |
| Organization ID, if applicable           | Links learning to CSO capacity profile |
| Learner role                             | Helps understand target group reach    |
| Course ID                                | Links learner to course                |
| Enrollment date                          | Tracks uptake                          |
| Language version                         | Tracks localization use                |
| Device type, if available                | Helps improve mobile-first design      |
| Location/region, if appropriate and safe | Helps understand geographic reach      |

Data collection should be minimal and safe. The platform should not
collect sensitive personal or political information unless clearly
necessary and protected.

## **Step 3: Track course progress and engagement**

The platform should monitor how learners move through the course.

| **Indicator**                | **What it shows**                             |
|------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Course start rate            | Whether enrolled learners begin the course    |
| Block completion             | Which blocks learners complete                |
| Drop-off points              | Where learners leave the course               |
| Time spent                   | Whether course length is realistic            |
| Resource downloads           | Which templates or job aids are useful        |
| Practice activity completion | Whether learners engage with applied tasks    |
| Final test access            | Whether learners reach the test               |
| Course completion            | Whether learners complete required components |

This helps DEC identify whether a course is too long, unclear, too
technical, or difficult to access.

## **Step 4: Track final test performance**

Every course has a final test. The platform should track both individual
and aggregated test results.

| **Test metric**            | **Meaning**                                          |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Number of attempts         | How many times learners take the test                |
| First-attempt score        | Initial level of mastery                             |
| Highest score              | Best learner performance                             |
| Pass rate                  | Percentage scoring 80% or above                      |
| Certificate rate           | Percentage scoring 90% or above                      |
| Question-level performance | Which questions are too easy, too hard, or confusing |
| Retake rate                | Whether learners need multiple attempts              |
| Feedback viewed            | Whether learners use feedback before retaking        |

The platform should apply the approved rule:

| **Score** | **Result**                                     |
|-----------|------------------------------------------------|
| Below 80% | Not passed                                     |
| 80%–89%   | Course passed                                  |
| 90%+      | Course passed and automated certificate issued |

The platform should clearly distinguish **pass**, **certificate**, and
**verified achievement** in dashboards.

## **Step 5: Track certificate issuance**

Certificates should be tracked automatically when a learner scores **90%
or above** on the final test.

Certificate tracking fields:

| **Field**                          | **Purpose**                                 |
|------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Learner ID                         | Identifies certificate holder               |
| Course ID                          | Links certificate to course                 |
| Course version                     | Shows which version was completed           |
| Final test score                   | Confirms eligibility                        |
| Certificate issue date             | Tracks award                                |
| Certificate ID / verification code | Supports authenticity                       |
| Capacity area                      | Links certificate to CSO taxonomy           |
| Indicator / standard, if shown     | Shows capacity relevance                    |
| Organization ID, if applicable     | Supports organization-level learning record |

The certificate should not be treated as proof of organizational
application. It confirms high final test performance only.

## **Step 6: Track practical proof submissions**

Where practical output is enabled, the platform should track submission
status.

| **Submission status** | **Meaning**                                                     |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Not submitted         | Learner has not submitted proof                                 |
| Draft saved           | Learner started but did not submit                              |
| Submitted             | Proof sent for review                                           |
| Under review          | Reviewer is assessing                                           |
| Revision requested    | Learner must revise or redact                                   |
| Accepted              | Proof meets criteria                                            |
| Rejected              | Proof does not meet criteria                                    |
| Unsafe / removed      | Submission contains unsafe data and must be removed or redacted |

Tracking practical proof is important because it shows whether learners
are attempting to apply learning to real or simulated CSO practice.

## **Step 7: Review and track verified achievements**

When practical proof is accepted, the platform may issue a verified
achievement, badge, or organization milestone.

Verified achievement tracking fields:

| **Field**                 | **Purpose**                                         |
|---------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Learner ID                | Identifies learner                                  |
| Organization ID           | Links achievement to CSO profile, where appropriate |
| Course ID                 | Links achievement to course                         |
| Practical output type     | Shows evidence type                                 |
| Reviewer decision         | Confirms acceptance                                 |
| Reviewer role             | Shows who verified                                  |
| Verification date         | Tracks award date                                   |
| Badge / achievement title | Shows recognition                                   |
| Capacity area             | Links to CSO capacity domain                        |
| Indicator / standard      | Shows capacity relevance                            |
| Visibility setting        | Controls learner/org/donor visibility               |
| Data protection status    | Protects raw evidence                               |

Verified achievement is different from a certificate:

| **Recognition**              | **Evidence basis**                    |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Certificate                  | Final test score of 90% or above      |
| Verified achievement / badge | Reviewed and accepted practical proof |

This distinction should remain visible in learner and organization
dashboards.

## **Step 8: Collect learner feedback**

The platform should collect short learner feedback after course
completion.

Feedback should be simple and practical.

Suggested questions:

| **Feedback question**                                     | **Response type** |
|-----------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
| Was the course relevant to your CSO work?                 | 1–5 scale         |
| Was the course easy to understand?                        | 1–5 scale         |
| Was the course usable on your device/internet connection? | 1–5 scale         |
| Did the examples feel realistic for your context?         | 1–5 scale         |
| What was the most useful part?                            | Short text        |
| What was confusing or difficult?                          | Short text        |
| What will you try to apply in your organization?          | Short text        |
| What support would help you apply this learning?          | Short text        |

Feedback should feed into course improvement and future capacity support
planning.

## **Step 9: Monitor course quality after publication**

DEC should monitor course performance after it is published.

Quality signals may include:

| **Signal**                               | **Possible interpretation**                               |
|------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| High drop-off early in course            | Course intro may be unclear or too long                   |
| Low final test pass rate                 | Content may be unclear or test too difficult              |
| High pass but low proof submission       | Learners understand content but need support applying it  |
| Many unsafe proof submissions            | Instructions or data protection guidance need improvement |
| Low mobile completion                    | Course may not be mobile-friendly                         |
| Low resource downloads                   | Resources may be hidden, unclear, or not useful           |
| Frequent learner confusion comments      | Blocks or examples need revision                          |
| Low certificate rate but high completion | Test may need review or more practice support             |

This monitoring supports course updates and version improvements.

## **Step 10: Update organization capacity profile where appropriate**

Where the platform includes organization profiles, results should be
aggregated carefully.

Organization-level records may include:

- number of learners enrolled;

- number of learners passed;

- number of certificates earned;

- number of verified achievements earned;

- capacity areas covered;

- indicators linked to verified proof;

- organization milestones achieved;

- pending practical submissions;

- recommended next courses.

Raw learner submissions or sensitive evidence should not be shared
widely. Organization admins may see appropriate summaries, while
donor-facing views should show only safe recognition summaries unless
the organization chooses otherwise.

## **Step 11: Generate dashboards and reports**

The platform should provide dashboards for different users.

## **Learner dashboard**

Shows:

- enrolled courses;

- progress;

- final test score;

- pass status;

- certificate status;

- practical proof status;

- verified achievements;

- recommended next steps.

## **Course creator dashboard**

Shows:

- enrollment;

- completion;

- drop-off points;

- final test performance;

- learner feedback;

- block-level issues;

- practical output submissions;

- revision suggestions.

## **DEC capacity dashboard**

Shows:

- capacity areas covered;

- indicators addressed;

- learner reach;

- organization reach;

- certificates issued;

- verified achievements awarded;

- practical proof patterns;

- priority gaps for future support.

## **Organization dashboard**

Shows:

- learners from the organization;

- course progress;

- certificates earned;

- verified achievements;

- indicator-linked capacity evidence;

- recommended next learning or support.

## **Donor/reporting dashboard, if enabled**

Shows safe aggregated evidence only:

- number of CSOs reached;

- capacity areas strengthened;

- courses completed;

- certificates issued;

- verified achievements awarded;

- indicators supported;

- selected anonymized learning insights.

Raw proof submissions should not be shared with donors without consent
and safeguards.

## **Step 12: Feed evidence into course improvement and adaptive planning**

The Monitoring and Evaluation Phase should not only report data. It
should support decisions.

Possible improvement actions:

| **Evidence**                                         | **Possible action**                                       |
|------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| Learners fail specific final test questions          | Revise question, improve explanation, add practice block  |
| Many learners stop at one block                      | Shorten, simplify, or redesign block                      |
| Learners submit weak practical proof                 | Add example, checklist, or proof guidance                 |
| Learners submit unsafe proof                         | Strengthen safety warnings and anonymization instructions |
| Low certificate rate                                 | Review final test difficulty and course clarity           |
| High demand for one capacity area                    | Prioritize more courses in that area                      |
| Organizations earn repeated achievements in one area | Mark capacity milestone or suggest advanced course        |
| Learners request mentoring                           | Route to non-course support where available               |

This creates a feedback loop from learner data back into course
improvement, future design, and DEC capacity strategy.

# **F. Platform Design Implications**

The Monitoring and Evaluation Phase should appear as a **learning
analytics and capacity evidence workspace**, not only a completion
dashboard.

## **1. Course Analytics Dashboard**

Tracks enrollment, progress, completion, pass rates, certificate rates,
drop-off points, and feedback.

## **2. Final Test Analytics**

Tracks question-level performance, attempts, pass rates, certificate
rates, and retake patterns.

## **3. Certificate Registry**

Stores issued certificates with certificate ID, learner, course, score,
date, and verification status.

## **4. Practical Proof Review Dashboard**

Tracks proof submissions, review status, reviewer decisions, revision
requests, and unsafe evidence flags.

## **5. Verified Achievement and Badge Registry**

Stores verified achievements linked to learner, organization, course,
capacity area, indicator, proof type, and review decision.

## **6. Organization Capacity Profile**

Aggregates safe organization-level learning and verified achievement
evidence.

## **7. Feedback and Course Improvement Log**

Collects learner feedback, reviewer observations, analytics issues, and
course revision decisions.

## **8. Data Safety and Visibility Controls**

Controls who can see learner data, proof submissions, organization
summaries, and donor-facing recognition.

## **9. Version-Based Reporting**

Ensures analytics are linked to the course version learners completed.

## **10. Adaptive Planning Dashboard**

Helps DEC identify priority capacity areas, high-demand topics, course
improvement needs, and future support pathways.

## **G. Final Output of the Monitoring and Evaluation Phase**

The phase produces several linked outputs.

| **Output**                            | **Purpose**                                                  |
|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Course analytics record               | Tracks enrollment, progress, completion, and drop-off        |
| Final test analytics record           | Tracks pass rate, certificate rate, and question performance |
| Certificate registry                  | Records automated certificates issued at 90%+                |
| Practical proof review record         | Tracks learner submissions and review decisions              |
| Verified achievement / badge registry | Records reviewed applied capacity evidence                   |
| Learner feedback summary              | Captures learner experience and application intent           |
| Organization capacity profile update  | Aggregates safe organization-level evidence                  |
| Course improvement log                | Documents revisions needed or completed                      |
| DEC capacity dashboard                | Supports strategic learning and reporting                    |
| Donor/reporting summary, if enabled   | Provides safe, aggregated capacity evidence                  |

# **Final Refined Tools and Templates for the Monitoring and Evaluation Phase**

To keep the phase practical, use five tools.

## **Tool 5.1: Course Analytics and Learner Progress Dashboard**

**Purpose:** Tracks enrollment, progress, completion, drop-off points,
final test access, and learner engagement.

**Output:** Course analytics record.

## **Tool 5.2: Final Test, Certificate, and Learning Achievement Report**

**Purpose:** Tracks final test performance, pass rates, certificate
rates, retake patterns, and question-level performance.

**Output:** Learning achievement report and certificate registry.

## **Tool 5.3: Practical Proof and Verified Achievement Tracker**

**Purpose:** Tracks practical proof submissions, review decisions,
verified achievements, badges, and organization-linked capacity
evidence.

**Output:** Practical proof and verified achievement record.

## **Tool 5.4: Learner Feedback and Course Improvement Log**

**Purpose:** Captures learner feedback, usability issues, application
intent, and course improvement decisions.

**Output:** Feedback summary and improvement log.

## **Tool 5.5: CSO Capacity Evidence and Reporting Dashboard**

**Purpose:** Aggregates safe evidence across learners, organizations,
courses, capacity areas, indicators, certificates, and verified
achievements.

**Output:** Organization-level and DEC-level capacity evidence
dashboard.

## **H. Monitoring and Evaluation Phase Quality Standard**

The Monitoring and Evaluation Phase is effective when it can show:

> who is learning, what they are completing, what they are passing, who
> is receiving certificates, who is submitting practical proof, what
> verified achievements are awarded, which CSO capacity indicators are
> supported, which courses need improvement, and what safe evidence DEC
> can use for adaptive learning, organizational recognition, and donor
> reporting.

The phase preserves the DEC traceability chain:

> Analysis evidence → capacity gap → baseline → root cause →
> intervention route → action map → learning design → flexible build →
> final test → certificate / verified achievement → review → publish →
> monitoring → evaluation → course improvement and capacity evidence.
