# Evidence Admissibility Checklist

Use this checklist before allowing an Analysis decision to unlock Design.

## Minimum admissibility questions

1. What is the capacity gap?
2. Who is the target learner group?
3. What is the current baseline or current practice?
4. What is the desired practice?
5. What source shows that this gap exists?
6. What root cause explains why the desired practice is not happening now?
7. Is the root cause Knowledge, Skill, Motivation, Environment, Mixed, or unclear?
8. If Mixed, what is the separable Knowledge/Skill component?
9. Is the gap linked to a DEC capacity area and sub-capacity where available?
10. Is there an indicator, standard, or internal capacity reference where available?
11. Are safeguards, no-harm, privacy, or civic-space risks recorded?
12. What evaluation anchor can later show whether the course helped?

## Evidence sufficiency levels

| Status | Meaning | Gate implication |
|---|---|---|
| Sufficient | Source evidence supports the gap, target learner group, root cause, and K/S or Skill route | May proceed if handover is complete and locked |
| Partial | Some evidence exists but important fields are missing | Do not unlock Design until clarified |
| Conflicting | Sources disagree on root cause, target group, or course-fit route | Require further diagnosis or reviewer decision |
| Missing | No evidence source is recorded | Block Design |
| Sensitive | Evidence includes safeguarding, civic-space, beneficiary, personal, or confidential organizational data | Apply minimization, redaction, and specialist review where needed |

## Acceptable source examples

- Approved CSO capacity assessment summary.
- Participatory scoping output.
- Training needs analysis summary.
- FGD/workshop synthesis.
- DEC or partner field observation summary.
- Document review finding.
- Donor or program review feedback.
- Existing course evaluation or learner feedback, if tied to a specific gap.
- Internal CSO capacity diagnosis record.

## Weak or insufficient source examples

- A generic course idea without evidence.
- A title such as "MEAL training" or "fundraising course" with no root cause.
- A donor requirement copied without local diagnosis.
- A leadership opinion without corroboration.
- A capacity area selected without a specific diagnosed gap.
- AI-generated problem statements that are not grounded in provided evidence.

## Privacy and safety checks

Before using evidence in UI or downstream design, classify it:

- Public / low sensitivity.
- Internal DEC/program evidence.
- Organization-confidential evidence.
- Learner/personally identifiable data.
- Practical proof or work sample.
- Safeguarding/protection-sensitive data.
- Civic-space/political sensitivity.
- Donor/compliance-sensitive data.

Default behavior:

- Do not expose raw sensitive evidence broadly.
- Use summaries instead of raw details where possible.
- Redact names, beneficiary details, case details, and sensitive locations when not needed.
- Do not send sensitive raw evidence into AI drafting prompts unless explicitly allowed and minimized.
